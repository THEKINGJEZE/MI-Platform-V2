## Core Philosophy

### Job Postings Are Warm Leads, Not Classifications

When we detect a job posting (direct or via competitor), we are not trying to decide "is this Agency or Managed Services?" We are identifying:

1. **A force with an active need** â€” they're hiring because they have a problem
2. **An opportunity to make contact** â€” the job posting is the hook
3. **A conversation to have** â€” let the dialogue determine the right solution

The job posting opens the door. Peel's value proposition is what we bring through it.

### We Present Our Full Capability

Every outreach presents Peel's solution framework, not a narrow service offer:

> "We help forces enhance operational capacity and build organisational capability through tailored, outcome-based solutions."

We never lead with "we have candidates" â€” that's a commodity conversation. We lead with the problem we solve and let the force determine which service fits their situation.

### Competitor Signals = Confirmed Opportunity

When a competitor posts a job for a police force:
- The force has a confirmed, active requirement
- The force has allocated budget
- The force is actively engaging suppliers
- **Peel should be part of that conversation**

This is not about displacement or criticising existing suppliers. It's about ensuring Peel is on the table while the force is actively looking.

### Principle: Contact the Problem Owner

The right contact is whoever owns the problem that the job posting signals â€” not necessarily who posted the job.

| Role Being Hired | Problem Domain | Target Contact |

### Contact Hierarchy

1. **Primary**: Problem owner (operational leader)
2. **Secondary**: Their deputy or direct report
3. **Fallback**: HR/Recruitment (if we cannot identify problem owner)

When contacting HR as fallback, the message should still present Peel's value proposition, not "we have candidates." HR can route us to the right conversation.

### Finding Contacts

**Sources (in order of reliability):**
1. Existing Peel relationships (check HubSpot)
2. LinkedIn (search force name + role title)
3. Force website (leadership team pages, FOI disclosure logs)
4. Police.uk / College of Policing directories
5. Conference speaker lists, publications, LinkedIn posts

**If contact not found:**
- Flag opportunity for manual research
- Use HR/Recruitment as interim contact
- Note in opportunity record: "Contact TBC â€” HR approach as interim"

## Follow-Up Cadence

### Standard Cadence (P2/P3 Signals)

| Touch | Timing | Channel | Content |
|-------|--------|---------|---------|
| 1 | Day 0 | Email | Initial outreach (templates above) |
| 2 | Day 3 | LinkedIn | Connection request + brief note |
| 3 | Day 7 | Email | Follow-up, add value (insight, case study) |
| 4 | Day 14 | Phone | Call attempt (leave voicemail if no answer) |
| 5 | Day 21 | Email | Final follow-up, leave door open |

**After 5 touches with no response**: Mark as "No Response" and move to nurture track (quarterly check-in).

### Accelerated Cadence (P1 Signals â€” Hot)

| Touch | Timing | Channel | Content |
|-------|--------|---------|---------|
| 1 | Day 0 | Email | Initial outreach |
| 2 | Day 0/1 | LinkedIn | Connection request + message |
| 3 | Day 1 | Phone | Call attempt |
| 4 | Day 2 | Email | Follow-up |
| 5 | Day 4 | Phone | Second call attempt |
| 6 | Day 7 | Email | Final follow-up |

**Rationale**: P1 signals indicate time-sensitive need. Faster cadence ensures we're in the conversation before decisions are made.

### Follow-Up Content Guidelines

**Touch 2-3: Add Value**
- Share relevant insight ("We've seen several forces tackle [X] by...")
- Reference case study (anonymised if needed)
- Offer something useful ("Happy to share how [similar force] approached this")

**Touch 4-5: Create Closure**
- Acknowledge they're busy
- Restate value briefly
- Leave door open ("If circumstances change...")
- Don't be pushy or desperate
